Development Timeline

Phase 1 — SRO
Robotic seeding: rovers, sensors, excavation demos. No crew. 10–20 t delivered.
Phase 2 — HRB
Short human visits: construction tests, limited ISRU, emergency shelter. 50–100 t.
Phase 3 — PSB
Permanent crew: shielded habitats, drilling, astronomy, polar ice ops. 300–800 t.
Phase 4 — IS
Industrial scale: bulk processing, propellant production, export manufacturing. 3,000–10,000+ t.

Physics & Formulas

Solar Array Sizing
A = P / (G × η × d)
Array area A needed for continuous power P, given solar irradiance G = 1361.6 W/m², panel efficiency η, and derating factor d for orientation losses.
Night Energy Storage
E = P × tnight × 24
Total energy E (kWh) to bridge the lunar night: continuous power P (kW) multiplied by night duration t in days. Battery mass = E / specific energy.
Thermal Rejection
Arad = Q / (ε σ T&sup4;)
Stefan-Boltzmann law: radiator area needed to reject waste heat Q at emissivity ε = 0.9, temperature T ≈ 300 K. No convective cooling on the Moon.
Regolith Shielding
m = ρ × d × Aroof
Shielding mass from regolith density ρ ≈ 1500 kg/m³, cover depth d, and habitat roof footprint. Target areal density ~200 kg/m² (20 g/cm²).
Pressure Loads
F = P × A = P × πr²
Bulkhead force from internal pressure P (70 kPa typical) acting on cross-sectional area. At r = 4 m: F ≈ 3.52 MN. Favors cylindrical/spherical geometry.
Life Support Mass Flow
mₑ = n × r × (1 - η) × t
Consumable mass for n crew at rate r (water: 2.5 kg/day, O&sub2;: 0.83 kg/day) with recycling efficiency η over mission duration t days.
Transport Energy (Delta-v)
Δvtotal ≈ 15 km/s
Representative Earth-surface to Moon-surface: 9.4 (LEO) + 3.2 (TLI) + 0.9 (LOI) + 1.6 (descent). Every unnecessary kilogram is punished exponentially via the rocket equation.
Delivered Cost
C = mtotal × ckg × L
Total cost from delivered mass times cost-per-kg ($10k–$20k adjustable assumption) times logistics overhead multiplier L. Imported shielding mass becomes economically punishing.
